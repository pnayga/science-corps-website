About Science Corps

Connecting PhD scientists with communities that need them most

Our Mission

Science Corps is a nonprofit organization that places PhD scientists as fellows in science education organizations around the world. Our fellows spend a minimum of six months living and working at partner institutions in underserved communities, teaching hands-on science, training local educators, and building lasting scientific capacity.

We believe that access to high-quality science education is a global imperative — and that PhD scientists have a unique and underutilized role to play in making that a reality. Science Corps bridges the gap between the world's most highly trained scientists and the communities that have the most to gain from their expertise.

Host Sites

Science Corps partners with organizations committed to hands-on science education in underserved communities.

CVIF-DLS

Center for Vivid Imaginations Foundation – De La Salle
Philippines

CVIF-DLS is a science education center in the Philippines that develops and implements innovative, hands-on science curricula for students who would otherwise lack access to quality STEM education. Science Corps has partnered with CVIF-DLS since 2019.

Aavishkaar

Aavishkaar Science Education Centre
Palampur, Himachal Pradesh, India

Aavishkaar is a science education center nestled in the Himalayan foothills, dedicated to making science accessible and engaging for students in rural India. Fellows at Aavishkaar lead workshops, develop materials, and work closely with local teachers. Science Corps has partnered with Aavishkaar since our early days.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Science Corps?
Science Corps is a nonprofit that places PhD scientists as fellows at partner science education organizations in underserved communities around the world. Fellows spend at least six months living and working at their host site, teaching hands-on science, training teachers, and building long-term scientific capacity.
Who can become a Science Corps fellow?
Science Corps fellowships are open to PhD scientists who completed their doctorate within the last four years. Fellows must be willing to commit to a minimum six-month placement at one of our partner institutions. We welcome applicants from all scientific disciplines.
Where do fellows go?
Our current partner host sites are CVIF-DLS in the Philippines and Aavishkaar in Palampur, India. We are always exploring new partnerships in communities that could benefit from a Science Corps fellow.
Is Science Corps a paid fellowship?
Science Corps provides fellows with a living stipend to cover basic living expenses at their host site. The stipend is calibrated to the local cost of living. Fellows are responsible for their own travel expenses to and from the host site.
How do I apply?
You can apply on our Apply page. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. We encourage interested candidates to reach out early and to read about our current fellows and host sites before applying.
Can organizations apply to become host sites?
Yes! If your organization works in science education in an underserved community and you're interested in hosting a Science Corps fellow, please contact us. We are always looking for mission-aligned partners.
