About the Fellowship

Science Corps fellows are recent PhD graduates in STEM who are placed at partner institutions in underserved communities around the world. Fellows design and teach scientific curricula, run hands-on experiments, mentor students, and participate in workshops that provide communities with direct access to world-class science education.

Fellowships can range from 3 months to one year, depending on the placement site and fellow availability. Science Corps coordinates logistics and provides support throughout the placement.

Eligibility

  • PhD degree in any STEM field (or anticipated completion by fellowship start date)
  • Strong commitment to science education and community engagement
  • Flexibility and adaptability for cross-cultural work environments
  • English proficiency (additional languages a plus)
  • Ability to travel and live abroad for the fellowship duration

Application Process

  • Step 1 — Express InterestEmail us at fellows@science-corps.org with your CV and a brief description of your research background and interest in Science Corps.
  • Step 2 — Application ReviewOur team will review your materials and reach out to schedule an interview if there is a good match with current placement opportunities.
  • Step 3 — InterviewA short video call with our team to discuss your experience, teaching interests, and placement preferences.
  • Step 4 — Placement MatchIf accepted, we will work with you to identify the best host site and fellowship duration based on your background and the needs of our partner institutions.
  • Step 5 — Fellowship BeginsCoordinate logistics, travel, and onboarding with our team. Your fellowship adventure begins!

What Fellows Say

"When I first began teaching at the Central Visayan Institute Foundation in Bohol, interacting with my shy high school students during our research methods class proved challenging. To encourage conversation, I custom-designed 'Science is for Everyone' stamps for the students' workbooks. Getting my students to open up about their own career interests allowed me to introduce one-on-one counseling sessions for those interested in scientific careers."
— Swastika Issar, Ph.D., Former Fellow (CVIF, Philippines)
"Bianca's adventure began with her participation in the 10th Jagna International Workshop. Here, she engaged with a diverse group of scientists, educators, and enthusiasts, sharing her insights and learning from others. One memorable moment was when she immersed herself in the local culture by learning to wear the 'malong,' a versatile Philippine garment."
— From Bianca Baldassarri's Fellow Update, 2024
